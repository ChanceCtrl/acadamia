## What is the electric field at p due to a small charge element of length dx at x?

$$\lambda = \frac{Q}{l}$$
$$dq = \lambda dl$$

### Net e-field
$$E = k_{e} \int \frac{dq}{x^{2}} = k_{e} \lambda \int_{a}^{(l+a)} \frac{dx}{x^{2}}$$

$$E = \frac{k_{e}Q}{a(l+a)}$$

# A uniformly charged ring of radius a and charge Q

$$\lambda = \frac{Q}{2\pi a}$$

$$dE = k_{e} = \frac{dq}{r^{2}} = k_{e} \frac{dq}{x^{2} + a^{2}}$$

$$E = \frac{k_{e}x}{(a^{2} + x^{2})^{\frac{3}{2}}}Q$$
