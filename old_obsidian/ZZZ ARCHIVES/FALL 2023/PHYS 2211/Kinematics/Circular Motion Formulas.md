Formulas for [[Circular Motion]]

$$a^{->}_{avg}(t) = \frac{deltav^{->}(t)}{deltat}$$

Distance traveled
$$deltas = rdeltatheta = vdeltat$$

Angular velocity (w) is change of angle over time. It is defined as
$$w = \frac{deltatheta}{deltat} = \frac{dtheta}{dt}$$
or
$$w = \frac{2pi}{T} = 2piF$$
$$F = 1/secs$$

Wack shit
$$|v_{1}| = |v_{2}| = v    =>    |deltav| = vdeltatheta$$
$$|a_{cp}| = \frac{|deltav|}{|deltat|} => |a^{->}_{cp}| = \frac{v^{2}}{r}$$

Ultimately that gives you
$$|a| = \frac{v^{2}}{r}$$

The radical component is nothing but $$a_{r}=\frac{v^{2}}{r}$$
The tangential component is simply: $$a_{t}=|\frac{deltav}{deltat}| = |\frac{dv}{dt}|$$

Some other wacky shit
$$a_{tot}=sqrt(a^{2}_{t}+a^{2}_{r})$$
